About TechWorksLab

Built by people who do the clinical work

We are a technology company for life sciences. Our teams come from clinical programming, biostatistics, and data engineering, so the software we build reflects how the work is actually done.

Who we are

TechWorksLab works with pharmaceutical, biotech, and clinical research organizations to build and run the technology behind their studies. We focus on the parts of drug development where good software makes a measurable difference: data standards, analytics, reporting, and submissions.

We are deliberately specialized. Rather than serving every industry, we have put our energy into clinical research, where the standards are exacting and the stakes are high. That focus shows in the depth of our work.

At a glance

  • Global deliveryTeams across the Americas, Europe, and Asia Pacific.
  • Clinical focusDrug development, trials, and regulatory submissions.
  • Built to standardsGxP, 21 CFR Part 11, HIPAA, GDPR, and CDISC.
  • Long term partnerWe support what we build across the study lifecycle.

How we work

A few principles guide every engagement.

Understand first

We learn your standards, your data, and your process before we propose anything. The right answer depends on context that no template can supply.

Build to standards

Traceability, documentation, and validation are part of the design from day one, not added at the end to satisfy an audit.

Make it reproducible

Pinned environments and version control mean a result can be regenerated and defended years after the study closes.

Work alongside teams

We sit with your statisticians, programmers, and data managers, and we leave them able to run what we deliver.

Move studies forward

Speed matters when patients are waiting. We cut the time spent on repetitive work so teams can focus on judgment.

Protect the data

Clinical and patient data is handled with the access controls, encryption, and care that the work requires.

  • Data standardsSDTM, ADaM, and define.xml.
  • ReportingTables, listings, and figures.
  • SubmissionsReproducible packages for authorities.

A partner, not a vendor

The difference shows up over time. A vendor delivers a product and moves on. A partner stays through the questions that come up at database lock, the change in standards halfway through a program, and the inspection that arrives without much warning.

We measure our work by whether your studies move faster and your filings hold up. When they do, we have done our job.
